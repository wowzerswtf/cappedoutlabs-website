Brand Overview

Capped Out Labs is an AI transformation firm built by operators, for operators. We deploy production revenue infrastructure into businesses generating $500K–$50M+ in annual revenue.

We don't deliver decks. We build systems that close deals.

Positioning Statement

Capped Out Labs is the only AI firm that thinks in exits. Every system we deploy is anchored to a revenue metric — close rate, LTV, churn, cash collection speed — because AI-systematized businesses command higher exit multiples. We were built by operators who learned to build AI. Not the other way around.

Core Beliefs

BeliefWhat It Means
Revenue-first framingEvery system is anchored to a revenue metric. If it doesn't move a number, it doesn't ship.
Production, not prototypesRunning systems with documentation, trained staff, and a 90-day handoff. Done means your team uses it daily and the numbers are moving.
Exit-prep architectureRevenue tied to systems is an asset. Revenue tied to people is a liability. Acquirers pay premiums for systems, not headcount.

Usage Rules

  • •Always render the full name: Capped Out Labs — never abbreviate to "COL" in external materials
  • •On dark backgrounds: white wordmark. On light backgrounds: navy wordmark.
  • •Minimum clear space: the height of the "C" on all sides
  • •Never distort, rotate, add drop shadows, or place on visually busy backgrounds

Voice & Tone

Capped Out Labs speaks operator-to-operator. Not consultant-to-client. Not vendor-to-buyer. We are peers who happen to have built something they need.

Voice Attributes

Direct
Say it in one sentence. No preamble. Lead with the point.
Not rude or dismissive
Confident
We've done this. The numbers prove it. State facts.
Not arrogant or condescending
Revenue-Fluent
Speak in close rates, LTV, churn, ROAS, exit multiples.
Not jargon-heavy or academic
Production-Minded
Running systems, live data, trained staff, handoffs.
Not theoretical or strategic-only
Honest
Acknowledge when AI fails. Name the problem. Then solve it.
Not defensive or evasive

Language Rules

The $200K+ Buyer Test

Would a business owner doing $3M–$50M in revenue read this and think "these people understand my world"? If it reads like a blog post for beginners — rewrite it.

Do / Don't

The quick-reference guardrails for anyone creating materials for Capped Out Labs.

Do

  • Lead with numbers and proof
  • Speak in revenue language (close rate, LTV, ROAS, churn, exit multiples)
  • Show live systems and real dashboards
  • Write like you're talking to someone who runs payroll
  • Use "we built" and "we deployed" — active, past tense, proven
  • Keep it tight — every word earns its place
  • Use JetBrains Mono for every number and metric
  • Use Lucide outline icons only

Don't

  • Use stock photography or generic AI imagery
  • Write in passive voice ("results were achieved")
  • Use consultant jargon (leverage, synergy, paradigm)
  • Oversell or fabricate metrics
  • Mix Labs and Media branding
  • Use light font weights in headings
  • Add decorative elements that don't serve comprehension
  • Use emojis in professional materials
